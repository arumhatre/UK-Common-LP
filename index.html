Types of Psoriasis

Plaque Psoriasis

This is the most common type, characterised by red, scaly patches ranging from 1 to 10 centimetres in size. These thickened patches of skin that can be itchy and painful and usually occur on the scalp, elbows, knees, and lower back

Guttate Psoriasis

Typically found in young people, this type of psoriasis is often triggered by a bacterial infection and appears as small, pinkish-red spots on the skin

Pustular Psoriasis

This type of psoriasis forms pus-filled blisters on the skin, which can be painful and cause fever and chills. It can occur anywhere on the body, but it's most commonly found on the palms of the hands and soles of the feet

Inverse Psoriasis

Inverse psoriasis primarily affects skin folds such as those in the groin or armpits, it tends to produce smooth patches of red, inflamed skin

Erythrodermic Psoriasis

This severe form of psoriasis can be life-threatening, as it covers the body with severe redness and scaling, often accompanied by intense itching and pain

Nail Psoriasis

This type of psoriasis affects the nails, causing them to become pitted, discoloured, and thickened. It can also cause the separation of the nail from the nail bed, and in severe cases, the nail may crumble or even fall off

Scalp Psoriasis

Scalp Psoriasis presents as red, scaly patches on the scalp, often accompanied by itching and sometimes extending beyond the hairline. It can also cause silvery-white scales, dandruff-like flakes, and hair loss in severe cases

FAQs

What is Psoriasis?

Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune skin condition which causes rapid buildup of skin cells, leading to red, itchy, and scaly patches. These patches can vary in size and severity and may appear on any part of the body

What are the main causes of Psoriasis?

The exact cause of Psoriasis is not fully understood, but it is believed to be a result of a combination of genetic, immune system, and environmental factors. Triggers can include stress, infections, certain medications, and injuries to the skin

What is the age at which one suffers from Psoriasis?

Psoriasis can develop at any age, but it most commonly appears between the ages of 15 and 35. It affects both genders equally and can occur in people of all races and ethnicities

What are the signs and symptoms of Psoriasis?

Common signs include red patches of skin covered with thick, silvery scales, itching or burning sensations, dry, cracked skin that may bleed, and stiff, swollen joints in psoriatic arthritis

How to cure Psoriasis permanently?

  4. Lifestyle Changes: A healthy diet, stress management, and avoiding triggers can help control flare-ups
  5. Moisturizers: Regular use keeps the skin hydrated, reducing scaling
  6. Medications: Topical treatments, oral medications, and biologics reduce inflammation and slow skin cell production

How does the progression of Psoriasis vary among individuals?

Progression varies greatly among individuals and can be influenced by factors such as genetics, overall health, stress, trauma, and response to treatment. While some may experience mild symptoms that come and go, others may have severe and persistent symptoms

Can Psoriasis spread to others?

Psoriasis is not contagious and cannot be spread from person to person through physical contact or other means. It is an autoimmune condition caused by internal factors rather than external contagions
